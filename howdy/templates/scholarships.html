Coca-Cola Scholars Program

The Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation gives out 150 scholarships each year to high school seniors! Valued at $20,000, the money can be used for any expense within 4 years.

Requirements:
  • Transcript
  • School profile
  • Contact information
  • You must list any organizations you have been involved in outside of school.
  • You must also list any awards or honors you have received.

    • The Posse Foundation

    This organization works to help students who may not have the strongest academic capabilities, but who demonstrate a wide range of other skills. The Posse Foundation believes in diverisfying college campuses, and giving under-represented students who want to go into STEM a chance to fulfill their dreams. Once you are accepted into the program, you will be put into a study group in college, with mentors throughout your four years. To top it all off, the organization then provides the students with full scholarships to partnered colleges!

    Requirements:
  • You must be nominated by an adult, who must be validated through a separate process.
  • Fill out the application on their website
  • Report SAT scores, transcripts
  • You will have to have an interview



    • Raise.me

    This website gives you money simply for your grades! If you end up attending one of their listed colleges, the money you've earned can be applied to your tuition.

    Requirements:
  • Simply create an account, enter your grades, and you're set!
  • You also get more money for inviting friends to join!

    • Gates Millenium Scholars

    Funded by the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, the Gates Millenium Scholars program works to provide minority students with signficant financial aid to help them further their education.

    Requirements:
  • You must belong to one of the following minority groups: African American, American Indian/Alaska Native, Asian Pacific Islander American or Hispanic American
  • They are specifically looking for students who excel in academics, have strong leadership skills, and who are in great need of financial aid.
